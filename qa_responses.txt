1. Outcome -Based Success Criteria and Measurement Framework  
How does JA define measurable success for the BizTown simulation redevelopment —both in 
terms of student learning outcomes (e.g., financial literacy gains, civic engagement indicators) 
and operational performance (e.g., simulation uptime, staff efficiency)?  Will JA partner with us to 
establish baseline metrics during Discovery and track them through pilot and beyond launch?  
JA Response: JA defines measurable success for the JA BizTown simulation redevelopment 
across both student learning outcomes and operational performance.  
From a student impact perspective, our primary goal is for participants to leave their JA BizTown 
experience with a deeper understanding of teamwork, how businesses operate, and the value of 
every role within an organization. We aim to see measurable growt h in financial literacy at both 
the personal and business levels, as well as increased confidence in applying those concepts in 
real-world scenarios. Additionally, we prioritize outcomes related to character development, 
including ethical decision -making, responsibility, and effective collaboration. These outcomes 
may be assessed through a combination of pre - and post -experience evaluations, educator 
feedback, and student self -reflection.  
From an operational standpoint, success is defined by a seamless and reliable simulation 
experience. This includes minimal to no system lag, strong and stable connectivity between 
student devices and administrative controls, and smooth integration across a ll components of 
the simulation. Efficiency for staff is also critical, with intuitive systems that streamline facilitation 
and reduce manual intervention.  
JA fully intends to partner with the selected vendor to establish baseline metrics during the 
Discovery phase and collaboratively define key performance indicators. We expect to track 
these metrics through pilot implementation and beyond launch to ensure c ontinuous 
improvement and long -term success.

JA Response: JA Areas currently operate with a high degree of flexibility in how their JA 
BizTown simulations are configured. Local sites are able to customize nearly all aspects of their 
experience, including business types, job roles, and individual job tasks. In add ition, Areas can 
adjust operational variables such as tax rates, student salaries, scheduling elements (e.g., 
break timing and structure), and other simulation parameters.  
In practice, most JA Areas make updates to their configurations a few times per year, primarily 
during the summer and the next largest window being over holiday breaks in the winter. These 
changes are typically driven by factors such as onboarding new busi ness sponsors, introducing 
or refining job roles and tasks, or correcting issues identified during program delivery. Areas are 
typically customizing 100 -150 job roles within the simulation. While there is significant variation 
across sites today, we do not  currently experience frequent conflicts related to national updates, 
largely because those updates have historically been limited.  
As part of the redevelopment, however, JA’s goal is to move toward a more standardized and 
consistent national experience, while still preserving the ability for local Areas to incorporate

7. Real -Time Transaction Architecture and Network Resilience  
The RFP emphasizes real -time transaction synchronization across student checking accounts, 
business accounts, financial institutions, and dashboards —all operating over facility WiFi that 
may be unreliable. Can JA clarify expectations for transaction consis tency in degraded network 
conditions? Should the system support local -first or offline -capable operation with eventual 
synchronization, or is persistent connectivity assumed? Additionally, what are the performance 
benchmarks for transaction throughput duri ng peak activity (e.g., shopping breaks with 150 
students transacting simultaneously)?  
JA Response:  JA recognizes that reliable real -time transaction processing is critical to the JA 
BizTown simulation, particularly during high -activity periods, and that facility WiFi conditions 
may vary.  
The system is expected to support offline -capable or “local -first” operation with eventual 
synchronization. Student devices should be able to continue key activities (e.g., purchases, 
deposits, payments) even in the event of temporary connectivity disrupti ons, with data syncing 
seamlessly once connectivity is restored. At the same time, JA is not expecting or requiring a 
traditional on -premise/local server solution; vendors should propose architectures that balance 
resilience with a cloud -based or hybrid ap proach.  
From a consistency standpoint, the system should prioritize accuracy and reconciliation across 
all entities, including student checking accounts, business accounts, and financial institution 
dashboards, once synchronization occurs. The user experience shou ld remain smooth and 
intuitive, with minimal visible disruption during transient network issues.  
In terms of performance, JA expects the platform to handle peak activity levels efficiently. At a 
typical facility, there may be approximately 150 students participating, with around 30 students 
transacting simultaneously during shopping periods. Within a 10-minute window, this can result
