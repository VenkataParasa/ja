INTRODUCTION   
Founded in 1919, Junior Achievement® (JA) is the oldest, largest, and fastest growing nonprofit 
economic education organization in the world. It is the largest partnership linking the private sector with 
education efforts. Junior Achievement’s educational programs serve as the primary  
vehicle to advance the organization’s purpose and vision: To inspire and prepare young people to 
succeed in a global economy. Programs are regularly developed, revised, and enhanced as Junior 
Achievement helps young people understand the economics of life.  
Junior Achievement USA has a nationwide presence. It provides content and services to 98 local JA Areas 
across the country. The JA Areas work with more than 220,000 Volunteers who deliver the content to 
over 4.6M students.  
Junior Achievement ensures the effectiveness and relevance of its economic and business education 
programs by utilizing validated curricula and instructional materials, specific program implementation 
standards, teacher and volunteer training, and program outcomes evaluations.  
Additional information about Junior Achievement and its activities can be found at https://www.ja.org/  
and internationally at https://www.jaworldwide.org/.  
 
SUMMARY OF PROJECT   
Project Goal:   
Engage with a software development vendor to build a new elementary/middle school simulation software to be 
used in a  hands -on, interactive physical experience where students apply classroom learning by operating 
businesses, managing money, and fulfilling  civic roles in a realistic, simulated community for a day in a physical 
facility. The physical simulation flow will be shared out by Junior Achievement.

When citizens understand taxation and spending, they better grasp civic responsibility. St ronger visual 
dashboards and real -time feedback tools will make these relationships tangible, helping students internalize the 
circular flow of the economy in ways that are relevant and memorable.

Redesign Vision and Core Features of the JA BizTown Simulation:  
The JA BizTown program introduces upper elementary and middle school students to the world of work, 
business management, and personal finance through an engaging blend of classroom instruction 
(curriculum ) and a hands -on, interactive simulation (field -trip st yle). The curriculum includes key 
instructional units —community and economy, work readiness, financial literacy, and business 
management —culminating in a fully immersive experience where students run an entire town. The 
simulation can take place in a permanent  JA facility or a mobile setup (similar to a pop -up trade show 
exhibit) hosted in schools, community centers, or other shared spaces.  
JA BizTown is a highly interconnected, real -time simulation environment in which approximately 80 to 
150 students operate a fully functioning economy for the day. Each student is assigned a specific role 
within one of roughly 14 to 20 businesses, with 6 –8 students per business. Every participant functions 
simultaneously as an employee, consumer, voter, and citizen within the simulated community.  
JA is reimagining the JA BizTown simulation to create a more dynamic, interconnected, and educational 
experience that better reflects today’s workplaces and technology. The new design will include the 
following key features:  
• A unified digital platform that adapts to both onsite and mobile delivery, offering flexible 
levels of complexity appropriate for elementary and middle school learners.  
• Engaging modern simulation that supports the physical simul ation  where students take on 
jobs, manage businesses. The software will feel real to them (but age appropriate)  
• A storefront -based economy where students manage operations, serve customers, pay 
expenses, and interact with other businesses to keep their town running smoothly.  
The simulation system must support a complex web of interdependent financial and operational 
transactions. Students earn varying wages based on their assigned roles, apply for and manage loans 
through financial institutions, maintain individual bank account s, and conduct purchases across 
businesses within the town. Each transaction, whether payroll distribution, loan disbursement, retail 
purchase, or service payment, must update in real time across multiple systems. For example, when a 
student makes a purcha se, the transaction must immediately deduct funds from the student’s personal 
bank account, reflect in the receiving business’s account, and record accurately within the financial 
institution’s system. This interconnectedness is foundational to maintaining  the integrity and authenticity 
of the economic simulation.  
The redevelopment should strengthen and modernize this integrated ecosystem. Key areas of 
opportunity include enhanced dashboards for students and staff, improved point -of-sale (POS) systems, 
robust banking functionality, and transparent financial reportin g tools. The system should provide clear, 
real -time visibility into individual, business, and community -level financial performance while 
maintaining data accuracy and stability under simultaneous usage.  
Additionally, the platform should support a one -device -per-student model to expand interactivity and 
instructional value. This includes capabilities such as digital voting, online loan applications, dynamic 
financial tracking, and digital marketing creatio n and publication within the simulated environment. The 
infrastructure must enable these features while preserving seamless cross -system communication.  
Given the depth of integration required across financial systems, business operations, civic engagement 
components, and digital content tools, the selected vendor must demonstrate strong full -stack 
development expertise and experience building scalable, tr ansaction -based systems with real -time data 
synchronization.

Background on JA BizTown  
JA BizTown is one of Junior Achievement’s Experiential Learning Center programs. Junior Achievement’s 
Experiential Learning Center programs combine practical applications in real world situations with 
principles learned in the classroom. Students discover how an economy functions by taking on real -world 
roles as citizens and employees, managing businesses, personal finances, and community responsibilities 
in an interactive, simulated town.  
 
JA BizTown gives 5th –6th grade students the opportunity to experience how a community operates and 
how personal and business finances are connected. After in -class lessons on topics such as financial 
literacy, business management, and community, students t ake on real -world roles as business leaders, 
employees, and consumers within a simulated town. They manage bank accounts, write checks, receive 
paychecks, and run their assigned businesses while providing goods and services to their peers. 
Throughout the d ay, students complete their job tasks, work to pay off their business loan, and see 
firsthand how their work contributes to the success of their businesses and community. Volunteers 
support students as they navigate their roles, offering insight and encour agement to make the experience 
both educational and inspiring.  
 
The on -site JA BizTown experience is a 4.5 -hour simulation led by JA staff where students operate an 
entire town, taking on roles as business owners, employees, and citizens. Throughout the day, they apply 
what they’ve learned in the classroom to make real -world business and personal finance decisions — 
running their assigned businesses, managing payroll, using debit cards, and participating in the town’s 
economy.  
Guided by volunteers and supported by an interactive digital platform, students experience firsthand how 
jobs, money, and community interconnect. This combination of a physical environment (the immersive 
town setting) and a cloud -based application allows students to seamlessly engage in business 
operations, banking, and communications using mobile devices. The result is a dynamic, hands -on 
experienc e that brings the world of work and free enterprise to life.

Simulation Overview  
 
In the JA BizTown experience, the student's objective is to develop work readiness by working in a 
simulated town with their own business, job, and job tasks.  
 
The steps begin with pre -simulation lessons covering work and career readiness, financial literacy, 
community and economy, and business management. During the pre -simulation lessons, students also 
apply, interview, and are hired for the jobs they will have  in the simulation.  
 
During the simulation, students work in one of the various businesses (aka storefronts) and in one of the 
various jobs within that business. They are paid for all of their hard work in the town and can use that 
money to shop and purchase items in JA BizTow n to take home.  
 
Student job tasks can be inside their own business, or they could be doing something in town or at 
another business. All businesses are interconnected within the JA BizTown economy. Supply and demand 
plays a huge factor in the success of the businesses in the town.  
 
The experience concludes with reflections about the success of the businesses and the challenges 
students faced and successes they experienced during the day.  
 
As students go through the simulation - they make the connections to transferable skills, how to grow 
within their organizations, and how to add value to their organizations.  
 
Audience Summary  
 
The audience for JA BizTown is elementary  and middle school students (grades 5-6; age 9 -12) across the 
country . Students complete a minimum of 12 lessons prior to arriving at a facility for their on -site 
experience. Students are "hired" for their job before they arrive at the facility, but typically do not know 
exactly what tasks they will be doing all day.  
Administrators (Simulation Managers) of JA BizTown set up  simulations, create templates, customize/ 
localize the experience to their community and local sponsors, create job roles and tasks for each 
business, and lead the students through the entire 4 hour simulation.

interface clean and easy to navigate. Make it clear where to focus attention – financial

preserved, it builds trust and reduces frustration. Persistence can be achieve d through 
features such as saved preferences, session continuity, and state retention.

Character Education Moments in Simulation  
o Honesty in Business Transactions and Reporting:  Students experience the importance of 
honesty by accurately recording transactions, reporting revenue and expenses, and approving 
payroll and invoices, understanding that transparency builds trust within a business and the 
broader community.  
o Responsibility for Business Resources:  Students learn to respect shared business resources 
by making thoughtful decisions about product pricing, inventory, staffing, and loan usage, 
recognizing that poor stewardship impacts a business’s sustainability.  
o Accountability for Financial Decisions:  JA BizTown reinforces accountability by requiring 
students to approve and authorize business decisions, such as paying bills and selling products, 
understanding that choices have real outcomes.  
o Ethical Business Practices and Fair Dealing:  Students are encouraged to engage in fair, 
ethical transactions with other businesses and citizens, learning that ethical behavior supports 
long -term success and community trust.  
o Thoughtful Use of Credit and Loans:  JA BizTown teaches responsible borrowing by helping 
students understand loans and encouraging them to work towards paying off their loans.  
o Balancing Individual Success with Community Impact:  JA BizTown emphasizes that 
business decisions affect more than just individual roles, encouraging students to consider the 
health of the town and its institutions.  
o Integrity in Role -Based Responsibilities and Value:  Students learn that different roles carry 
different responsibilities, and that fulfilling one’s role ethically contributes to overall success 
and value.  
o Citizenship Through Economic Participation:  Students experience the role of responsible 
businesses in supporting the broader community through participation in shared systems
